Why and How ZK-SNARK Works

About ZK-SNARKs

Zero-Knowledge Succinct Non-Interactive Arguments of Knowledge (ZK-SNARKs) are revolutionary cryptographic protocols that allow one party to prove to another that they possess certain information without revealing the information itself.

The interactive polynomial plotter helps visualize the mathematical foundations that make ZK-SNARKs possible.

Key Concepts

  • Polynomial Commitments and their role in ZK proofs
  • Homomorphic hiding and encryption schemes
  • Quadratic Arithmetic Programs (QAPs)
  • Trusted setup ceremonies
  • Succinct proof generation and verification

Applications

Real-World Uses

  • Privacy-preserving cryptocurrencies: Zcash, Aztec
  • Scalability solutions: zk-Rollups, StarkWare
  • Identity systems: Anonymous credentials
  • Verifiable computation: Outsourcing computations
  • Compliance: Regulatory-friendly privacy
